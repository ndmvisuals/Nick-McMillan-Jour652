When 5 year-old Hurunessa Fariad stepped off the plane into the streets of New York, the first thing she noticed was the absence of dust. Compared to Pakistan, where she and her family had been seeking shelter as refugees from the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan, this was a completely different world.

As the snow started to gently cover the streets outside her house in Queens, she remembers being confused but enthralled by the glowing balls of light that adorned every house. A thousand questions sprung to mind.

What is Christmas? What is Christianty? Who are these people?

Through talks with her father and siblings, she learned that there were people of other faiths here. Her dad explained how America was a place for people of all different backgrounds, a place where people come for refuge, and most importantly that this is the place where everyone and anyone can come for a better life.
